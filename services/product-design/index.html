Services · Product design

Design that survives engineering.

Most design dies in handoff - beautiful mockups meeting real data, real latency and real thumbs. At Mokshify design and engineering share one room and one definition of done, so what ships is what was designed.

How we design

  • Workflows before screens. We map what the user is trying to finish before drawing where buttons go - the order that prevents pretty products nobody can operate.
  • Prototype the risky part. The interaction where the product wins or dies gets a working prototype early; the settings page does not need one.
  • Real data in design. Layouts are tested with the longest name, the empty state, the thousand-row list - the conditions demo data hides.
  • Devices users actually hold. Thumb reach, interruption tolerance, modest phones on slow networks - the audience our markets actually are.
  • Accessibility in the definition of done. Contrast, keyboard paths and reduced-motion respect are review items, not a retrofit - the standard this site holds itself to.

What you get

Product flows and interface design, a working component system in code (not a Figma museum), interactive prototypes for the load-bearing moments, and design decisions recorded next to engineering ones - because “why does it work this way” deserves an answer in writing.

Common questions

Do you do design-only engagements?

Rarely, and honestly: our design is strongest fused with our engineering. If you only need mockups, a dedicated design studio may serve you better - we will say so on the call.

Can you work with our existing brand?

Yes - product design extends your identity into workflows and components; it does not replace it.

What does the cinematic homepage prove?

That we treat design as engineering: the film is design executed with performance budgets, accessibility fallbacks and published numbers.

Have a workflow users struggle to finish?

Describe where they give up. Design review within 24 hours.
